# Weekly Content Planning Workflow

## Trigger
Run by `/social-weekly`, recommended Sunday evening or Monday morning.

## Cadence Targets

| Platform | Weekly Target | Content Types |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| Twitter/X | 7-14 posts + daily engagement | Releases, tips, thoughts |
| LinkedIn | 2 updates + 1 thought piece | Updates, lessons, insights |
| Reddit | 1-2 posts | Show-off, launch, discussion |
| Dev.to | 0-1 article (2/month target) | Tutorial, how-I-built |
| Medium | 0-1 article (1/month target) | Narrative, long-form |
| Bluesky | 5-10 posts | Mirror best Twitter + original |
| HN | 0-1 submission | Only for notable releases |

## Planning Steps

### 1. Audit Last Week
- Count posts per platform
- Identify gaps (missed any platform targets?)
- Note which content got the most engagement

### 2. Source Content Ideas
- New releases/features shipped this week
- Interesting bugs fixed or technical challenges solved
- Industry trends or conversations to join
- Evergreen topics not covered recently

### 3. Assign to Days
Monday-Friday slots:
- Morning: Twitter post (release or tip)
- Midday: Engagement round
- Afternoon: LinkedIn (Tue/Thu) or Reddit (Wed)
- Evening: Bluesky mirror

Weekly one-offs:
- LinkedIn thought piece: draft Thursday, post Friday morning
- Dev.to article: write Wednesday, publish Thursday
- Medium article: write over the weekend (monthly)

### 4. Pre-Draft High-Priority Content
The LinkedIn thought piece takes the most effort. Draft it during the planning session so it's ready.

### 5. Save Plan
Write the full plan to `content-calendar.json` with status "planned" for each entry.
