# Dev.to Article Template

## Structure
Tutorial-style article. "Here's how I solved X" framing.

## Front Matter
```
---
title: How I {did specific thing} with {technology}
published: true
tags: typescript, cli, opensource, tutorial
series: (optional series name)
---
```

## Body Format
```
## TL;DR
{2-3 sentences summarizing what you'll learn}

## The Problem
{What you were trying to do and why existing solutions didn't work}

## The Approach
{Your solution at a high level}

## Step by Step

### 1. {First step}
{Explanation}

\`\`\`typescript
// actual code from your project
\`\`\`

### 2. {Second step}
...

## What Didn't Work
{Be honest about dead ends — this is the most valuable part}

## Results
{Concrete numbers: performance, lines of code, time saved}

## Source Code
{Link to repo, specific files/commits}
```

## Rules
- Use real code from your actual project, not contrived examples.
- Include what didn't work. Dev.to readers value honesty over polish.
- Max 4 tags. Pick the most specific ones.
- Link to specific files/commits in your repo, not just the repo root.
- Keep it under 8 minutes reading time (~2000 words).
- TL;DR at the top is mandatory for scanners.
